# Brand Guidelines — FINDGOD

> Define the FINDGOD brand voice, tone, and style. Claude follows these when writing any copy, content, or user-facing text.

## The One-Sentence Rule

**"Let scripture say the hard thing. Apply it to real modern struggles. Never punch down. Never go partisan."**

This single line is the filter. If a post violates it, don't post it.

---

## Who We Talk To

The 16-30 year old man who is:

- Lost, anxious, or overwhelmed
- Sick of corny Christian branding
- Quietly searching for meaning
- Tired of the noise, news, scrolling, and modern pressure
- Hungry for discipline, purpose, and brotherhood
- Ready to hear the truth delivered straight

He is the founder, years ago. Write to him. (See `.claude/docs/icp.md` for the full profile.)

---

## Brand Promise

**Strength. Wisdom. Brotherhood. Truth.**

Every post should serve at least one of those four.

---

## Voice Rules

### DO

- Short sentences. Cut everything you can cut.
- Quote scripture directly. Let it land on its own.
- Talk about real struggles: money, lust, anxiety, fear, purpose, fatherhood, ego, failure, addiction.
- Name the modern idols out loud: phones, comfort, validation, status, pleasure, followers.
- Be unafraid to say "this culture is lying to you."
- Make men feel convicted, called out, and challenged.
- Use active verbs and masculine imagery (war, forge, battle, path, discipline).
- Let silence do work. Blank space is allowed.

### DON'T

- No corny church language ("God's got this!", "blessed!", "real talk fam!")
- No fake positivity or half-smiling enthusiasm.
- No partisan politics (parties, candidates, elections).
- No attacking specific people or movements by name.
- No "us vs. them" tribalism.
- No shaming — challenge, don't belittle.
- No long theological explanations. Let the Word do the heavy lifting.
- No "bro" or "dude" language.
- No emoji spam. (One rare 🕊️ or ✝️ maximum.)

---

## Tone Reference

Think of it as: **Jocko Willink + Ryan Holiday + a monastery + an A24 war film.**

Direct. Ancient. Cinematic. Quiet confidence. Never sells itself.

---

## Words We Avoid

- "Blessed" (as filler)
- "Journey" (unless it's specific)
- "Amazing" / "incredible" / "awesome" (lazy filler)
- "Just" (as filler — "just keep going", "just trust")
- "Happy" / "joyful" (too soft for our voice — we use "peace," "rest," "strength")
- "Bro" / "Dude" / "Fam"
- "Real talk"
- "Hit different"
- Excessive emojis

## Sentence Archetypes (copy these structures)

- **Reframe:** "You weren't built for [modern comfort]. You were built for [biblical calling]."
- **Command:** "Scripture says: [verse]. Read it twice."
- **Lie vs. Truth:** "The lie: [modern narrative]. The truth: [scripture reference]."
- **Call to action:** "Stop [soft thing]. Start [hard thing]."
- **Redirect:** "If you're [struggle], open to [book chapter]."
- **Question:** "When was the last time you [biblical discipline]?"

---

## Examples — GOOD

> "You weren't made to be comfortable. You were made to be forged."

> "1 Corinthians 16:13. Read it until you believe it."

> "The world rewards weakness. The Kingdom rewards the strong who kneel."

> "Anxiety is a spiritual problem first. Start there."

> "You don't have a motivation problem. You have a purpose problem."

> "The Bible isn't a self-help book. It's a battle manual."

---

## Examples — BAD

> "God's got amazing plans for you, bro! 🙌💯"

> "Such a blessed day to follow Jesus!"

> "Real talk: God is moving in my life fam!"

> "Hope you have an incredible week ahead! Stay blessed!"

> "Just trust the process and let God work!"

---

## Final Test Before Posting

Before any post goes live, ask:

1. Does it serve Strength, Wisdom, Brotherhood, or Truth?
2. Could a lost 22-year-old man read it without rolling his eyes?
3. Is scripture doing the heavy lifting?
4. Is there anything corny, soft, or partisan in it?

If it passes all 4: post it.
If it fails any: rewrite.
