# FINDGOD — Strategic Action Plan (v2)

> The full action plan for FINDGOD. Approved by founder on 2026-04-14. Source of truth for execution.

## Context

**Why this exists:** The founder lived the problem. Raised Christian, but never knew Christ — until hardship, anxiety, depression, and business failures broke him down and Jesus brought peace. FINDGOD is built to reach the man HE was — the lost, anxious, overwhelmed 16-30 year old who is sick of corny Christian branding and looking for something real.

**Vision:** FINDGOD becomes "the Yeezy of faith" — a cinematic, premium, unapologetic masculine brand. Foundation is the AI Bible Companion app + content engine (months 0-6). Then influencer/creator amplification (months 6-12). Then — ONLY when the brand has earned the right — clothing capsules (month 12-18+), events, community, mentorship. Yeezy/Essentials model: brand precedes product.

**Ultimate goal:** Bring more men to Jesus Christ. Revenue is a byproduct of serving the mission.

## The Flagship Insight — The Long Loop (refined 2026-04-17)

Most Christian brands do ONE thing: either merch, or an app, or a community. FINDGOD builds a sequenced ecosystem where each layer earns the right to the next:

1. **The AI Bible Companion (foundation — months 0-6)** — free app where men ask anything and get biblical answers, daily devotions, and struggle-specific guidance. This is where the *emotional bond* is built. Hours of use per week. Walks them through their hardest moments. Becomes personal.

2. **Content + influencer trust (amplification — months 6-12)** — daily Instagram, email, and mid-tier creator partnerships. Not paid endorsements — creators who actually use the app and vouch honestly. Trust compounds.

3. **The clothing layer (extension — months 12-18+)** — premium dark editorial apparel. By this point, thousands of men have a real relationship with FINDGOD. They're not "considering buying" a hoodie — they've been waiting for it. The bond becomes *visible* in public.

**Why this sequence is the moat:**
- The app creates emotional attachment (hours of use per week, for months, before anything is sold).
- Influencers validate the brand through real testimonies, not ad reads.
- When merch finally drops, it feels assumed — not announced.
- Competitors can copy any single layer. They can't copy the sequence or the trust built at each step.

**Why nobody else has done it:** Christian app companies (Hallow, Glorify) are VC-funded and think like software companies. Christian streetwear brands (God Is Dope, etc.) are tee-shirt printers. Neither has the discipline to let the brand precede the product the way Kanye did with Yeezy and Jerry Lorenzo did with Essentials. FINDGOD does.

## Content Engine — 7 Instagram Pillars

1. **The Daily Verse** — One scripture, one cinematic image, every day. The anchor format (Daily Stoic playbook applied to scripture).
2. **Dead Men Still Speak** — Quotes from Christian warriors: Bonhoeffer, C.S. Lewis, Spurgeon, Augustine, Luther, Jim Elliot, Tozer, Whitefield.

4. **Truths They Don't Preach** — Sharp biblical takes on: pleasure, comfort, purpose, suffering, fatherhood, fear, lust, money. Edge pillar.
5. **Questions Real Men Ask** — Carousel posts: "What does the Bible say about…money? fear? your future? your dad?" Save/share pillar.
6. **The Call** — Short emotional voiceover Reels inviting lost men to come home. Evangelism pillar.
7. **Brotherhood** — Real men's testimonies (rolls in once the app/community launches). Proof-of-life pillar.

**Cadence:** 1-2 feed posts/day + 2-3 Reels/week minimum. Cross-post to TikTok + YouTube Shorts. 100% of the time.

## 3-Phase Execution Plan (refined 2026-04-17)

Three sequential phases. Each phase earns the right to the next. Clothing is explicitly deferred until month 12+.

### PHASE 1 — Foundation (Months 0-6)

Goal: build the emotional bond. No clothing. No paid community. No events. Just the product men use daily + the content that brings them in.

**Track A — Content & Audience**
- Daily IG post + email newsletter ("The Daily Word")
- Cross-post every piece to TikTok + YouTube Shorts
- Brand system locked (fonts, logo, palette) by end of week 2
- USPTO trademark filed under LLC (anonymity preserved)
- 30-day content bank ahead of posting cadence

**Track B — AI Bible Companion (THE flagship product)**
- MVP live at findgod.com within 2-3 weeks of brand lock
- Free chat: anyone can use it once without signup
- Signup required to continue — email + password (or magic link)
- Daily Question ritual (app asks YOU hard questions each morning)
- Streak tracking
- Scripture-of-the-day
- Tech: Next.js on Vercel · Anthropic Claude API · Supabase auth · Stripe (when premium launches)

**Success signals for Phase 1:**
- App in daily use by real men (not just founder's friends)
- First unprompted testimonies in DMs
- Email list in the thousands
- Zero merch exists

### PHASE 2 — Amplification (Months 6-12)

Goal: validate the brand through trusted voices. Turn daily users into evangelists.

**Track A — Content scaling**
- Refined playbook on what's working from Phase 1
- Testimony Tuesday reels with real users (with permission)
- Content bank operating 60 days ahead

**Track B — AI app revenue**
- Premium tier launches ($4.99/mo)
- Paid newsletter tier opens ($9/mo)
- Audio mode, struggle pathways, journal history added

**Track C — Influencer partnerships**
- 3-5 aligned creators (mid-tier, not mega-stars)
- Not paid ads — give them the app, let them use it, let them vouch honestly
- Creator partnerships structured as long-term, not one-off drops

**Success signals for Phase 2:**
- Premium subscribers in hundreds, then thousands
- Creators mentioning FINDGOD unprompted in their content
- Brand visible enough that strangers ask "what is FINDGOD?"
- Still zero merch

### PHASE 3 — Extension (Months 12-18+)

Goal: release the clothing only when it feels inevitable. Open community and event layers.

**Track C — First clothing capsule (quiet release)**
- 3-5 basics: heavyweight tee, hoodie, cap, crewneck
- No hype drop theater. No "DROP 001" countdown campaigns.
- Announced once to email list + IG. Orders flow.
- Small wordmark + hidden scripture reference only. Brand palette only.
- Print-on-demand to test demand (Printful/Gelato), then real manufacturer
- See Clothing Philosophy in business-bible.md for the full "given, not gimmick" rules

**Track D — Community + events**
- Paid Skool "Brotherhood" community launches
- First "Brotherhood Weekend" retreat (50-100 men)
- Mentorship network pilot with vetted Christian men

**Success signals for Phase 3:**
- Capsule sells out without hype-marketing
- First event sells out
- Multiple creator partnerships converting to long-term advocacy
- Premium tier revenue covers operating costs
- First testimonies: "FINDGOD brought me to Christ."

## Risks & Guardrails

- **Burnout:** Daily content is grinding. Stay 30 days ahead in a content bank. Never post live.
- **Aesthetic drift:** One off-brand post kills the feel. Strict visual rules in week 1.
- **Theology risk:** One bad take = cancelled. Stay scripture-forward. Bring on a theological consultant by month 3-4.
- **Trademark risk:** "Find God" is generic. Trademark "FINDGOD" wordmark immediately.
- **Platform risk:** Instagram can suppress Christian content. Build the email list HARD — own that audience.
- **AI app liability:** AI giving biblical answers can say wrong things. Disclaimers in app. Pastor reviews outputs periodically. "This is AI, confirm with scripture" footer.
- **Copycat risk:** If it works, copycats come fast. Moat = the combo (app + merch + content) + taste + consistency.
